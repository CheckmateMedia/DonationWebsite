What We Are Here To Do:

We are We Just Keep on Swimming. We are a nonprofit intending to collect donations for individuals with traumatic brain injuries. The founder, Matthew Brignola, got a brain injury in September of 2019. He knows the struggles of having a traumatic brain injury and whants others with brain injuries to feel included, normal, loved, and important. Frankly, having a brain injury is psychologically devastating. One of the hardest thing is accepting that you will never be exactly the way you once were. The goal of this organization is to empower those who have brain injuries.

The day Matthew got his brain injury he almost died. Matthew was Moutain biking on a ski mountain, crashed, broke his arm, and injured his brain. But luckily, an off duty medic had been riding on the trail right behind matthew and intubated him on site. Without that, Matthew might have died or suffered far worse brain damage from hypoxia, which is a lack of oxygen to the brain. Matthew survived. He is a survivor and intends to help others who have also survived. If you have a brain injury just know that you are not alone and the goal of this organization is to help us take care of one another.

Mission

We Just keep swimming was founded in Colorado, which is the state where Matthew Brignola got his traumatic brain injury. Denver, Colorado is also the home of Craig hospital, the number one hospital for treating victims of traumatic brain injuries. The goal of this organization is to empower others with brain injuries. Having a brain injury makes Matthew Brignola feel subhuman at times. He once was a teacher, read a textbook and became a tech support employee. He had worked hard and saved over $10,000. Because of his medical expenses ne has less than $5000 but still has too much money to qualify for government assistance. His bills keep coming in. The mission of we keep swimming is to empower other victims and to raise donations to minimize other's medical debt as well. We understand that it may seem hard to get back to a job and make money after getting a btraumatic brain injury, so are making an effort to teach victims how to code and create websites, just like how Matthew Brignola taught himself.

Why sink when you could just keep swimming?
